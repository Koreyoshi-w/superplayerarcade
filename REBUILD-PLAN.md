# Super Player Arcade — Revised Rebuild Plan (v2)

---

## 1. Website Structure

Six core pages, each with a clear purpose:

| Page | Purpose | Key Content |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Home** | First impression — tells the full story of a dual-experience destination | 50/50 hero, story-driven scroll, warm lifestyle tone |
| **Arcade** | The social energy of playing together | Winning moments, group reactions, photo ops, claw machines, prizes |
| **Creative Studio** | The aspiration of making something beautiful | Lifestyle imagery, emotional storytelling, 6 craft experiences |
| **Parties** | Group bookings & event packages | Birthday parties, corporate events, group sessions, custom packages |
| **Pricing** | Transparent pricing for all activities | Arcade pricing, studio activity pricing, party packages, bundles |
| **Contact** | Location, hours, booking, inquiries | Map, address, phone, email, booking form, social links |

**Navigation:** Sticky top nav, logo left or center, all six page links visible. Mobile: hamburger opens full-screen warm cream overlay with large tap targets.

## 2. Homepage — The Experience Journey

The homepage is not a stack of sections. It is a story. The visitor arrives, discovers two worlds, explores each one, imagines themselves there, and decides to visit.

---

### Act 1 — ARRIVE: The Split Hero

**This is the most critical section on the entire site.**

The hero is a true 50/50 split. Not a hint. Not a tease. Two complete, equal halves that each stand on their own.

**Layout:** Full viewport width and height (or near-full). Divided cleanly down the middle — left half and right half. On mobile, stacked vertically as two full-width blocks.

**LEFT HALF — ARCADE**
- Background: warm lifestyle photo of friends at claw machines, cheering, holding prizes
- Overlay: subtle warm gradient so text reads clearly
- Its own headline: *"Play Together"*
- Its own subline: *"Claw machines, prizes, and the kind of fun you'll talk about all week"*
- Its own CTA button: **"Explore the Arcade →"**
- Visual mood: energetic, social, golden lighting, laughter

**RIGHT HALF — CREATIVE STUDIO**
- Background: warm lifestyle photo of hands creating — decoden, paint, colorful materials on a table
- Overlay: subtle warm gradient so text reads clearly
- Its own headline: *"Make Something Beautiful"*
- Its own subline: *"Decoden, painting, tufting, and crafts you'll actually want to keep"*
- Its own CTA button: **"Visit the Studio →"**
- Visual mood: aesthetic, calm, tactile, warm tones, focused hands

**Center element (optional):** A small shared logo or tagline badge sits at the vertical divider — something like the Super Player Arcade logo or a line that says *"Two experiences. One destination."*

**What this is NOT:**
- Not a single hero image with two buttons
- Not one side bigger than the other
- Not a text-only hero
- Not a video background
- Not dark, not neon, not black

**Mobile behavior:** The two halves stack vertically. Arcade on top, Studio below. Each block is at least 50vh tall so both get full visual presence.

---

### Act 2 — DISCOVER: Two Worlds, One Place

A short breathing moment after the hero. One warm centered headline, like:

*"Where play meets creativity"*

Then two large panels side by side (not cards — panels). Each panel is generous, taking about half the viewport width, with a large lifestyle image, a short emotional paragraph (not bullet points), and a soft CTA.

**Arcade panel copy direction:** Focus on the feeling — the moment you grab the joystick, the way everyone crowds around the claw machine, the victory scream when you win. This isn't about listing machines. It's about what it *feels like* to be there with your people.

**Studio panel copy direction:** Focus on the aspiration — the satisfaction of making something with your hands, the colors spread across the table, the moment you hold up something you made and think *"I did that."* This isn't a class list. It's an invitation to create.

---

### Act 3 — EXPLORE THE STUDIO: Aspirational, Not a Grid

**This section does NOT present activities as a 6-item feature grid.**

Instead, it tells the story of creative exploration through a flowing editorial layout:

**Opening:** A warm headline like *"Find Your Kind of Creative"* followed by one short paragraph that sets the tone — something about how every activity is designed so anyone can walk in with zero experience and leave with something they're proud of.

**Activity showcase:** A staggered, magazine-style layout. Not a uniform grid. Think editorial spreads, not product cards.

Each activity gets:
- A large, beautiful lifestyle image (not a product shot — show hands working, materials in motion, finished pieces being admired)
- The activity name in the display font
- A 2–3 sentence emotional description — not "what it is" but "what it feels like"
- A subtle tag or badge (e.g., "Most Popular", "Great for Groups", "New")

**Example tone for Cream Glue / Decoden:**
Not: "Decoden is a Japanese craft technique using cream glue to decorate phone cases."
Instead: "Layer, squeeze, arrange, and sparkle. Pick your case, load up on charms, and build something so extra your friends will ask where you got it."

**Example tone for Tufting:**
Not: "Tufting allows you to create custom rugs using a tufting gun."
Instead: "There's something deeply satisfying about punching yarn into canvas and watching your design come to life, row by row. Take it home. Put it on your wall. You made that."

The layout alternates — one activity's image is on the left, the next on the right. This creates visual rhythm and avoids the "product listing" feel.

End with a soft CTA: **"See All Studio Experiences →"**

---

### Act 4 — FEEL THE ARCADE: Social Energy, Not Gaming Specs

**This section is about emotion, not equipment.**

It does NOT list machines or game types. Instead, it captures the *social energy* of the arcade.

**Layout:** Full-width immersive section. A large background image or a collage of lifestyle moments — friends huddled around a claw machine, someone holding up a big stuffed prize with a huge grin, a group high-fiving, someone posing with their haul.

**Copy direction — moments, not features:**
- The anticipation when the claw drops
- The eruption when someone wins
- The pile of prizes on the table at the end of the night
- The photos you'll post before you even leave

**Structure:**
- A warm headline: *"The Kind of Fun That Gets Loud"*
- 3–4 "moment" vignettes — short, punchy, image-paired descriptions of real emotional moments (not feature descriptions)
- Each vignette is a feeling: "The Win," "The Crowd," "The Haul," "The Photo"
- CTA: **"See the Arcade →"**

**What this is NOT:**
- Not a list of claw machines and game types
- Not specs or equipment descriptions
- Not dark/neon styled — stays warm and lifestyle-toned
- Not isolated from the rest of the page's visual language

### Act 5 — IMAGINE: Parties & Group Experiences

The visitor has now seen both worlds. This section plants the seed: *"What if I brought my whole group here?"*

**Layout:** A warm highlighted section (slightly deeper cream background, like `#EED6B3`) with a different visual rhythm to break the scroll.

- Headline: *"Bring Your Whole Crew"*
- Subline: *"Birthday parties, team outings, friend dates — we'll plan everything"*
- 3 simple selling points with warm icons:
  - "Private party rooms"
  - "Arcade + Studio combos"
  - "Custom packages for any group size"
- One large lifestyle photo of a party in progress — balloons, crafts on the table, friends laughing
- CTA: **"Plan a Party →"**

---

### Act 6 — TRUST: Social Proof & Guest Gallery

The visitor now thinks *"This looks amazing."* This section confirms it with real proof.

- Headline: *"See What Our Guests Make (and Win)"*
- A curated mosaic gallery — not a uniform grid. Mixed sizes. Some images tall, some wide. Alternating between:
  - Finished craft creations (decoden cases, tufted rugs, painted figures)
  - Arcade wins (prize hauls, claw machine victories)
  - Group photos and candid moments
- Optional: pull from Instagram with a #SuperPlayerArcade tag
- The gallery should feel like scrolling through a friend's photo dump — casual, real, joyful
- No captions needed — the images speak

---

### Act 7 — PLAN: Visit Us

The final section before the footer. The visitor is now ready to come.

- Headline: *"Come Hang Out"*
- Address, hours, phone — clean and scannable
- A warm illustrated or styled map embed
- Two buttons: "Get Directions" / "Contact Us"
- This section flows directly into the footer — no hard divider

### Footer

- Warm dark brown background (`#5B3822`) with cream text — the only dark section on the entire page

## 3. Design System — Light-First Manifesto

### Absolute Rules

These are non-negotiable and apply to every page, every section, every component:

1. **No dark full-page sections** (except the footer)
2. **No black backgrounds anywhere**
3. **No neon colors anywhere**
4. **No arcade/gaming UI patterns** (no pixel fonts, no scanlines, no glowing borders, no dark gradients)
5. **No tech startup aesthetic** (no dark hero, no glassmorphism, no gradient meshes)
6. **The entire site lives in the cream/warm spectrum**

### What It Should Feel Like

Think: a warm cafe with beautiful things on the walls. Think: a lifestyle brand Instagram account. Think: the kind of place where the lighting is always golden hour.

Not: a gaming website. Not: a Chuck E. Cheese site. Not: a SaaS landing page.

### Color Palette

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| `cream-light` | `#F6E6CC` | Primary page background |
| `cream` | `#F3E1C7` | Alternate section backgrounds |
| `cream-warm` | `#EED6B3` | Highlighted sections, borders, hover states |
| `orange-accent` | `#FFC56E` | Primary CTAs, highlights, badges, active states |
| `brown-dark` | `#5B3822` | Headings, primary text, footer background |
| `brown-medium` | `#7A5635` | Body text, captions, secondary elements |
| `warm-white` | `#FFFDF9` | Card surfaces, input backgrounds |
| `orange-deep` | `#E8A84C` | Button hover states |

**Background alternation pattern:** Sections alternate between `cream-light` and `cream` to create subtle depth without introducing dark areas.

### Typography

- **Display / Headings:** Rounded, friendly serif — *Fraunces* (primary candidate) or *DM Serif Display*
- **Body:** Clean sans-serif — *DM Sans* (primary candidate) or *Plus Jakarta Sans*
- **Accent (optional):** For callouts or badges — *Quicksand* or *Baloo 2*
- Base size: 16px. Scale ratio: 1.25 (major third).

### Shape Language
- Rounded corners: 12px–20px on cards, 24px+ on buttons (pill shape)
- Soft shadows: `0 4px 20px rgba(91, 56, 34, 0.08)`
- Wavy/curved section dividers (SVG) instead of straight lines
- Organic decorative shapes (soft blobs, rounded badges)
- No sharp edges anywhere

### Iconography
- Rounded line-style icons, warm brown stroke
- Phosphor Icons (rounded set) or custom simple illustrations
- 24px base size

### Photography Direction
- Golden-hour warmth — everything looks like late afternoon sunlight
- Real people: hands crafting, friends laughing, genuine reactions
- Overhead/flat-lay for craft materials and finished pieces
- Candid energy for arcade moments
- Color-graded to sit naturally within the cream palette

### Motion
- Gentle fade-in-up on scroll (subtle, not dramatic)
- Soft hover lifts on interactive elements
- Smooth section transitions
- No flashy animations, no arcade-game effects in the UI

## 5. Component Architecture

### Tech Stack
- **Framework:** Next.js 14+ (App Router)
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS with custom design tokens
- **Language:** TypeScript
- **Fonts:** Google Fonts via `next/font`
- **Icons:** Phosphor Icons (rounded)
- **Animation:** CSS transitions + optional Framer Motion for scroll reveals
- **Deployment:** Vercel or any static host

### Key Components

**Layout Shell:**
- `Navbar` — sticky, warm cream, transparent-to-solid on scroll, logo + 6 links + "Book Now" CTA
- `Footer` — warm brown with cream text, the only dark element
- `SectionWrapper` — handles max-width, padding, background alternation, and wave dividers
- `MobileMenu` — full-screen warm cream overlay

**Homepage Sections (each is its own component):**
- `HeroSplit` — the 50/50 split hero, each half independent
- `DualWorlds` — two large panels, emotional copy
- `StudioStory` — editorial alternating layout for 6 activities
- `ArcadeEnergy` — immersive social moments section
- `PartyInvite` — highlighted booking teaser
- `GuestGallery` — mosaic photo grid
- `VisitUs` — location, hours, map

**Shared Primitives:**
- `Button` — primary (orange pill), secondary (cream outline), ghost
- `SectionHeading` — display font title + optional subtitle
- `WaveDivider` — SVG curve between sections
- `Badge` — small tags ("Popular", "New", "Book Now")
- `ActivityFeature` — image + emotional copy, alternating left/right layout
- `MomentVignette` — small image + punchy moment description

## 6. Content Strategy

### Voice & Tone
- Warm, inviting, conversational — like a friend telling you about their favorite spot
- Aspirational but accessible — "you could do this, and it would be amazing"
- Not corporate, not childish, not salesy
- Short sentences, emotional hooks, sensory language

### Content Principles

1. **Show the feeling, not the feature.** Don't list what's available — describe what it's like to experience it.
2. **Every section earns the next scroll.** Each section should make the visitor curious about what comes next.
3. **Two identities, one warmth.** Arcade copy can be more energetic. Studio copy can be more calm. But both live in the same warm, premium tone.
4. **Real over polished.** Guest photos over stock photos. Candid moments over posed shots. Real reactions over marketing language.

### Messaging by Experience

**Arcade messaging keywords:** winning, reactions, group energy, loud fun, prizes, "did you see that?!", photo-worthy moments, friendly competition, everyone crowds around

**Studio messaging keywords:** satisfying, beautiful, "I made this", hands-on, colorful, aesthetic, take it home, no experience needed, your own creation, therapeutic, focused flow

### Image Strategy
- Hero: two distinct lifestyle photos that each could stand alone as a brand image
- Studio activities: warm, close-up, hands-in-motion photography
- Arcade: candid, mid-action, social group photography
- Gallery: a mix of both — curated to feel like a real Instagram feed
